Spiritual Heritage

The Seven Saints
of Marrakech

Seven scholars. Seven shrines. One sacred pilgrimage that has united the faithful for centuries.

☽

The Rijal Sab'a

The Rijal Sab'a — "The Seven Men" — are the patron saints of Marrakech, revered Islamic scholars and Sufi masters who lived and taught in the city between the 11th and 17th centuries. Every year, thousands of pilgrims perform the Ziara, a sacred circuit visiting each of the seven shrines in a single day, seeking blessings, healing, and spiritual renewal. This tradition, initiated by Sultan Moulay Ismail in the 17th century, remains one of Morocco's most profound living rituals.

Meet the Seven Saints

1

Sidi Youssef Ben Ali

سيدي يوسف بن علي

12th Century

The most venerated of the seven, Sidi Youssef Ben Ali was a leper who withdrew from society to devote himself entirely to God. Known for his extraordinary patience and piety, he is buried south of the medina and his shrine is always the first stop of the Ziara pilgrimage.

📍 South of the medina · Bab Aghmat area
2

Sidi Ayyad

سيدي عياض

11th – 12th Century

Cadi Ayyad was one of the greatest Islamic scholars of the medieval period — a judge, theologian, and author of the renowned Al-Shifa, a definitive biography of the Prophet Muhammad. Born in Ceuta, he was exiled to Marrakech where he died and was buried.

📍 Near Bab Aghmat · Southern medina
3

Sidi Abu al-Qasim al-Suhayli

سيدي أبو القاسم السهيلي

1114 – 1185 CE

An Andalusian scholar of language and hadith, Sidi Abu al-Qasim al-Suhayli settled in Marrakesh in his later years. Despite his great scholarly stature, he lived simply and humbly, and students would travel from afar to learn from him. He is buried near Qadi Iyad in the southern medina.

📍 Southern medina · Near Qadi Iyad
4

Sidi Bel Abbès

سيدي بلعباس

12th – 13th Century

The patron saint of Marrakech, Sidi Bel Abbès as-Sabti is the most beloved figure in the city's spiritual life. He dedicated his life to serving the blind and the poor, distributing food every evening at sunset. His grand shrine in the northern medina remains an active centre of charity and devotion to this day.

📍 Northern medina · Sidi Bel Abbès quarter
5

Sidi Abdel Aziz

سيدي عبد العزيز

14th – 15th Century

Sidi Abdel Aziz al-Tebaa was a devout scholar and Sufi who spent his life in worship and teaching in Marrakech. Known for his asceticism and deep spirituality, he is remembered as a teacher of teachers — his influence shaped generations of Moroccan scholars.

📍 Central medina
6

Sidi Al-Ghazwani

سيدي الغزواني

15th – 16th Century

Known as "Moulay al-Ksour," Sidi al-Ghazwani was a prominent Sufi scholar celebrated for his wisdom and spiritual authority. He was a student of Al-Jazouli and became one of the most influential figures in the spread of Sufism throughout Morocco.

📍 Western medina
7

Sidi Mohammed Ben Slimane al-Jazouli

سيدي محمد بن سليمان الجزولي

15th Century

A revered Sufi master and poet, Sidi Mohammed Ben Slimane al-Jazouli was the founder of the Jazouliyya Sufi brotherhood. He authored the celebrated Dala'il al-Khayrat, a collection of prayers upon the Prophet that is still recited across the Muslim world today.

📍 Medina · Near Bab Doukkala

The Ziara Pilgrimage

The Ziara (زيارة — "visit") is a sacred one-day circuit of all seven shrines, traditionally performed on foot through the winding streets of the medina. Initiated by Sultan Moulay Ismail in the 17th century as a way to unite the city's spiritual life, it draws pilgrims from across Morocco and beyond every year.

🚶 How the Ziara Works

1
Begin at dawn at the shrine of Sidi Youssef Ben Ali, the most venerated of the seven saints.
2
Walk the medina streets in a specific order, visiting each shrine, reciting prayers and offering blessings.
3
At each mausoleum, pilgrims light candles, burn incense, and ask the saint for intercession.
4
Complete the circuit at the grand shrine of Sidi Bel Abbès, the city's beloved patron saint.

⭐ When to visit: The grand annual Ziara takes place in the Islamic month of Rajab. However, individual shrines are open year-round for personal visits, and the atmosphere around the Sidi Bel Abbès quarter is always alive with devotion and activity.

"He who walks the Ziara walks in the footsteps of a thousand years of faith, humility, and the unbroken love of a city for its saints."
— Moroccan Proverb
